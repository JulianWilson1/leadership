Power

Power is a relational term. It is a relationship between human beings in specific historical, economic, and social setting-it must be exercised to be visible. There are several different definitions of power: Power is having legitimate access to systems sanctioned by the authority of the state. Ronald chrisom and Michael Washington in undoing racism philosophy of international social change the peoples institute for survival and beyond second edition 1997 page 36 Power is the ability to define reality and to convince other people that it is their definition. Dr. Wade Nobles used at an undoing racism workshop of the peoples institute Power is ownership and control of the major resources of state and the capacity to make and enforce decisions based on that ownership and control. CWS Power is access to those individuals social groups, class and institutions which own or control the resources of the state. CWS
